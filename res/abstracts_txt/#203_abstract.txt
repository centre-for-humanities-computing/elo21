"Feeling without Touching" is a remote workshop inspired by "The Dictionary of Obscure Sorrows" by
John Koenig, a list of invented words that describe feelings that “give a name to emotions we all might
experience but don’t yet have a word for.” Workshop participants will engage in a series of (dis)embodied
activities that refer to the body, emotion, and a speculative concept, "feelosophy." All participants will
explore and be involved in creating a collection of poetic intimate objects that hold pre/post-verbal
